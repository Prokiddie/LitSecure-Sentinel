# LitSecure Sentinel — Go-To-Market Strategy
**June 2026 | Internal Strategy Document**

---

## 1. Market Entry Strategy: Government-Led, Sector-Expanded

The fastest and most defensible path to market is through **government adoption first**. A MACRA or MACERT deployment creates:
- A national reference customer (highest credibility)
- Regulatory endorsement (other sectors feel pressure to adopt)
- A working case study for development partner funding
- Data collection at national scale (strengthens AI models)

```
Phase 1: Government → Phase 2: Telco/Banking → Phase 3: NGO → Phase 4: SADC
```

---

## 2. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary ICP: Government Cybersecurity Unit
- **Who:** MACRA (Malawi Communications Regulatory Authority), MACERT, Ministry of ICT, MRA, Anti-Corruption Bureau
- **Problem:** No centralized incident tracking; manual reporting via email/phone; no cross-sector visibility
- **Decision Maker:** Director of ICT / Chief Information Security Officer
- **Budget Holder:** Head of Department / Ministry Finance
- **Buying Trigger:** Cyberattack on government systems; regulatory audit; World Bank digital governance conditions
- **Sales Cycle:** 4–8 months
- **Contract Value:** $12K–$60K/year

### Secondary ICP: Telecom Operator
- **Who:** Airtel Malawi, TNM Malawi
- **Problem:** SIM swap fraud causing millions in losses; no automated cluster detection
- **Decision Maker:** Head of Fraud Management / Chief Security Officer
- **Buying Trigger:** Spike in SIM swap complaints; MNO regulatory directive
- **Sales Cycle:** 2–5 months
- **Contract Value:** $30K/year

### Tertiary ICP: Development Partner / NGO
- **Who:** UNDP, USAID implementers, World Bank Digital Malawi projects
- **Problem:** Need to demonstrate cybersecurity capability for digital project compliance
- **Decision Maker:** Programme Manager / ICT Consultant
- **Buying Trigger:** Digital project grant requirement; capacity-building mandate
- **Sales Cycle:** 1–3 months
- **Contract Value:** $8K/year

## 3. Sales Channels

### Channel 1: Direct Government Outreach
- Target: MACRA, MACERT, Ministry of ICT ICT4D Unit
- Approach: Request for Pilot meeting → Demo → Proof of Concept → MOU → Contract
- Tools: Executive Summary, Security Whitepaper, Pilot Agreement Template
- Key Contact: Director of Cybersecurity at MACRA

### Channel 2: Events & Speaking
- **Malawi ICT Trade Fair** (annual): Demo booth + speaking slot
- **COMESA CyberDrills** (regional): Present as Malawi's national CERT tool
- **ISOC Malawi Chapter** events: Thought leadership on incident reporting
- **Academic Partnerships**: University of Malawi, MUBAS — student projects, awareness

### Channel 3: Development Partner Networks
- World Bank Digital Development Partnership
- USAID Southern Africa Digital Initiative
- GIZ Digital Transformation Centre Malawi
- Approach: Grant proposal + technical brief + impact metric framework

### Channel 4: Telecom Sector
- Direct outreach to Airtel Malawi Fraud & Security team
- TNM Digital Services division
- Reference: "We already detect SIM swap clusters — here is a live demo"

## 4. Sales Playbook

### Stage 1: Awareness (Month 0–2)
- [ ] Launch public demo instance at `demo.litsecure.mw`
- [ ] Publish Executive Summary on LinkedIn (founder's profile)
- [ ] Submit press release to Malawi24, Times Digital, Nyasa Times
- [ ] Contact MACRA ICT Director directly (email + LinkedIn)
- [ ] Submit NRDF Innovation Grant application

### Stage 2: Engagement (Month 2–4)
- [ ] Host live demo with MACRA technical team (45-minute session)
- [ ] Deliver Pilot Agreement Template; propose 90-day free pilot
- [ ] Follow up Airtel and TNM fraud teams with SIM swap detection demo video
- [ ] Present at Malawi ICT Forum (if date aligns)

### Stage 3: Conversion (Month 4–8)
- [ ] MACRA pilot deployed and active
- [ ] Weekly feedback session with pilot users
- [ ] Document 3 measurable outcomes (incidents reported, response time, fraud detected)
- [ ] Convert pilot to paid contract ($12K minimum)

### Stage 4: Expansion (Month 8–18)
- [ ] Use MACRA as case study for banking/telecom pitches
- [ ] Land second government customer (MACERT or MRA)
- [ ] Close first telecom contract (Airtel or TNM)
- [ ] Apply for World Bank / UNDP project funding
- [ ] Reach $100K ARR milestone → angel investor conversations

## 5. Pricing Strategy

**Principle:** Price for value captured, not cost of delivery.

### Anchor Effect
Always show Enterprise ($60K) first. Government Starter ($12K) then feels affordable.

### Pilot-to-Paid Conversion
- Offer first 3 months at 50% to reduce barrier
- After 90 days: full annual contract with month-1 payment

### Volume Discounts (Year 2+)
- 3-year contract: 15% discount
- Multi-ministry deal: 20% discount on 3+ ministry contracts

### NGO Pricing
- Standard 60% discount off Government Starter ($4,800/year)
- Funded by development partner programs, not NGO budgets

## 8. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Government procurement delay (12+ months) | High | Parallel NGO + development partner sales |
| Budget unavailability | Medium | Structure as grant-eligible with development partner co-funding |
| Competitor enters market | Low | Local knowledge + AI learning flywheel creates defensible moat |
| Technical failure during demo | Low | Maintain dedicated demo environment, pre-recorded backup |
| Key-person dependency | High | Document all processes; hire technical co-founder in Year 1 |
